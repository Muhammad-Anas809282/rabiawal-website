Birth & Childhood

The Light of Makkah ﷺ

The Blessed Birth

12th Rabi-ul-Awwal, 571 CE

Makkah al-Mukarramah

On the blessed night of 12th Rabi-ul-Awwal in the Year of the Elephant (571 CE), the greatest light ever to illuminate the earth was born in the holy city of Makkah. The birth of Muhammad ﷺ was accompanied by miraculous signs that foretold his extraordinary destiny as the final messenger of Allah.

His mother, Aminah bint Wahb, reported experiencing divine dreams and visions during her pregnancy, and on the night of his birth, it is said that lights appeared that could be seen as far as Syria.

The Blessed Childhood

Early Years

Muhammad ﷺ was orphaned at an early age. His father Abdullah died before his birth, and his mother Aminah passed away when he was six years old.

Grandfather's Care

After his mother’s death, he was cared for by his loving grandfather, Abdul Muttalib, who recognized his special nature and noble character.

Uncle Abu Talib

When Abdul Muttalib died, his son Abu Talib took care of Muhammad ﷺ and raised him with love and protection throughout his youth.

Noble Character

Even as a child, Muhammad ﷺ was known for his honesty, trustworthiness, and noble character, earning him the title “As-Sadiq Al-Amin”.

The Year of the Elephant

The Prophet ﷺ was born in the famous "Year of the Elephant" (Am al-Fil), when Abraha, the Abyssinian ruler of Yemen, attempted to destroy the Kaaba with a massive army that included war elephants. Allah protected His sacred house by sending flocks of birds (Ababil) that pelted the army with stones of baked clay, as mentioned in Surah Al-Fil. This divine protection occurred in the same year our beloved Prophet ﷺ was born, signifying the importance of his coming into this world.

"Have you not seen how your Lord dealt with the companions of the elephant? Did He not make their treacherous plan go astray?" - Quran 105:1-2

Call to Prophethood

The First Revelation ﷺ

"Read! In the Name of your Lord Who created. Created man from a clot."
- Quran 96:1-2 (First Revelation)

The Cave of Hira

Jabal an-Nour, Makkah

Before receiving his first revelation, Muhammad ﷺ would often retreat to the Cave of Hira on Jabal an-Nour (Mountain of Light) for contemplation and worship. This practice of spiritual reflection prepared him for his great mission.

At the age of 40, during one of these spiritual retreats in the month of Ramadan, Angel Jibril (Gabriel) appeared to him with the first verses of the Quran, marking the beginning of his prophetic mission.

The First Command: "Iqra!"

The very first word revealed was "Iqra" (Read/Recite), emphasizing the importance of knowledge, learning, and literacy in Islam.

The Momentous Event

The Angel Jibril appeared to Muhammad ﷺ in a form that filled the horizon and commanded him to read. When Muhammad ﷺ replied that he could not read, the angel embraced him tightly three times, each time repeating the command.

"ٱقْرَأْ بِٱسْمِ رَبِّكَ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ * خَلَقَ ٱلْإِنسَٰنَ مِنْ عَلَقٍ * ٱقْرَأْ وَرَبُّكَ ٱلْأَكْرَمُ * ٱلَّذِى عَلَّمَ بِٱلْقَلَمِ * عَلَّمَ ٱلْإِنسَٰنَ مَا لَمْ يَعْلَمْ"

"Read! In the Name of your Lord Who created. Created man from a clot of blood. Read! And your Lord is Most Generous. Who taught by the pen. Taught man what he did not know."

- Quran 96:1-5

Support of Khadijah RA

After this overwhelming experience, Muhammad ﷺ returned home trembling and sought comfort from his beloved wife Khadijah RA. She immediately believed in him and provided unwavering support during these crucial early days.

Khadijah RA took him to her cousin Waraqah ibn Nawfal, a learned Christian scholar who confirmed that Muhammad ﷺ had indeed encountered the same angel that came to Moses (Musa) AS, and that he was destined to be a prophet.

"By Allah, Allah will never disgrace you. You keep good relations with your kith and kin, help the poor and the destitute, serve your guests generously and assist the deserving calamity-afflicted ones."

- Khadijah RA to her husband

The Mission of Guidance

Divine Guidance

Muhammad ﷺ was chosen to guide humanity from darkness to light, bringing the final message of monotheism.

The Quran

The revelation of the Quran continued for 23 years, providing complete guidance for all aspects of human life.

Universal Message

His message was not limited to Arabs but was meant for all of humanity until the Day of Judgment.
